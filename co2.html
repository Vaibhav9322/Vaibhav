Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

Back to Home

Overview

Carbon dioxide (CO₂) is one of the most important greenhouse gases released by human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas. CO₂ is naturally present in the atmosphere, but human activities have significantly increased its concentration.

Sources of CO₂

  • Combustion of fossil fuels
  • Deforestation
  • Industrial processes
  • Natural processes like volcanic eruptions

Global Warming Potential (GWP)

CO₂ has a global warming potential (GWP) of 1, meaning it serves as the baseline measure for other greenhouse gases.

Environmental Impact

Increasing concentrations of CO₂ in the atmosphere contribute to climate change, leading to rising global temperatures, melting ice caps, and extreme weather events.
